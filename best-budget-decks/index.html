Overview: building on a budget in the June 2026 meta

Sparks are PvZH's crafting currency — you earn them by recycling cards you don't need and spend them to craft new ones. A deck's spark cost is simply the total to craft every card in it, so a lower number means a cheaper build and a faster path to a competitive ladder deck. This top 10 collects the strongest decks you can assemble without breaking the bank, spanning both sides of the lawn: eight plant lists and two zombie lists across seven heroes.

Leafy Pot on Captain Combustible takes the crown at a 95.05 composite score and a perfect 100 synergy — a genuine top-meta aggro deck you can mostly craft from commons and rares. From there it's a balance of price and power: the cheapest entries are Burst Flare on Solar Flare (≈5,200 sparks), Budget Agro on Captain Combustible (≈6,200), and Budget Flag Swarm on Z-Mech (≈6,800), which together make the perfect first decks for new players on either faction.

What are Sparks in PvZH?

Sparks are the crafting currency in Plants vs. Zombies Heroes. You earn them by recycling cards you don't need and spend them to craft new ones. A deck's spark cost is the total to craft every card in it, so a lower number means a cheaper build.

How are these budget decks ranked?

Each deck is scored across four pillars — performance, consistency, synergy, and curve health — then graded and ordered by composite value. Spark cost is shown alongside for budget context but is not one of the scored pillars.

How many cards are in a PvZH deck?

A Plants vs. Zombies Heroes deck contains exactly 40 cards, with a maximum of 4 copies of any single card. Every budget list in this guide follows that format.
