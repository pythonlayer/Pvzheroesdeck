Overview: Impfinity in the June 2026 meta

Impfinity is the hero version of Imp and the leader of the Sneaky and Crazy classes. His signature superpower, Triple Threat, spawns two Impfinity Clones — a fitting emblem for a hero who wins by swarming the board with cheap, hard-hitting zombies before the opponent stabilizes. He owns more gravestone cards than any other hero and is famously the only one who can assemble the full dancing combo, but he pays for that flexibility with weak late-game staying power and poor card draw.

This month his best builds lean on the conjure engine to solve exactly those weaknesses. Dino-Conjure leads at 88, ramping through Barrel of Barrels, Dr. Spacetime, and Laser Base Alpha into Tankylosaurus and Raiding Raptor while generating a steady stream of cards. Below it sit gravestone-dino hybrids, imp-swarm tempo (Gizzard Swarm), tanky midrange, and the pure-tribal Pogo Mug Binary Flamenco dancing deck. Only two builds reach A this month; the rest are solid B-tier brews for players who want variety.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best Impfinity deck right now?

As of June 2026, Dino-Conjure is the highest-rated Impfinity deck in our database with an 88 composite score and an A grade. It's a conjure-value engine that ramps into Tankylosaurus and Raiding Raptor through Barrel of Barrels, Dr. Spacetime, and Laser Base Alpha.

What classes and playstyle does Impfinity have?

Impfinity leads the Sneaky and Crazy classes and is the hero version of Imp. His signature superpower, Triple Threat, makes two 2/1 Impfinity Clones. He swarms the board with cheap, hard-hitting zombies, owns the most gravestone cards of any hero, and tends to struggle in the late game with limited card draw.

Can Impfinity play a dancing deck?

Yes — he's the only hero who can build the full dancing combo, using Aerobics Instructor to boost Line Dancing Zombie and Conga Zombie before dropping a game-ending Flamenco Zombie. The Pogo Mug Binary Flamenco deck in this guide is that build.

Does Impfinity have an S-tier deck?

Not this month. Impfinity's best lists top out at an A grade, with Dino-Conjure leading at 88, and most builds land in B. He's a strong tempo and swarm hero but currently sits below the S-tier cutoff.
